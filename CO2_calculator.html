Simple carbon calculator

Home emissions

Your home is the source of emissions via its space heating and water heating system. Space and water heating is generally separated, as the rooms are often heated by a central heating system (for instance radiators that are heated on a separate water circuit, heated by a furnace -burning fuel or gas-), and the hot water system then being another separate water circuit, heated by an electric, gas or fuel-fired boiler. There are also setups that have a single (combined) circuit though, but this is more rare. Whatever the case, if you use an electric heater for either one, you'll need to calculate the emissions out using the "electricity carbon calculator" below.

You might also have additional (non-electric) space heaters, such as a fireplace or wood stove.
If you have a fireplace, wood or coal stove, you also need to calculate the emissions of that -in case you have not replanted the same amount of trees as what you've burned that year in your fireplace or wood stove-.

Space heating

m³ of natural gas X 11,41 kWh (*1) = kWh X 0,6kg CO2/kWh = kg CO2

l of number 2 fuel oil X 2,8 kg CO2/l (*2) = kg CO2

m³ of wood X 750 kWh (*3) X 1,5 kg CO2 per kWh= kg CO2

kg of coal X 8 kWh X 1 kg CO2 per kWh (*4)= kg CO2

Water heating

If you have an electric boiler, calculate using the electricity carbon calculator below.

For gas-fired boilers:

m³ of natural gas X 11,41 kWh (*1) = kWh X 0,6kg CO2/kWh = kg CO2

Notes

*1: There is 11,41 kWh in 1 m³ of natural gas (compressed at the regular pressure in gas distribution networks)
*2: There is 3,15 kg CO2 in 1 kg (=1,12l -see http://www.endmemo.com/cconvert/kgl.php -) of fuel oil; see http://numero57.net/2008/03/20/carbon-dioxide-emissions-per-barrel-of-crude/ ; this figure also matches roughly with the data from https://www.epa.gov/energy/greenhouse-gases-equivalencies-calculator-calculations-and-references
*3: There is 750 to 1000 kWh in 1 m³ of wood (variation depends on wood species; see http://www.fao.org/3/a-i4441e.pdf )
*4: There is 8 kWh in 1 kg of coal; see https://www.euronuclear.org/info/encyclopedia/coalequivalent.htm

Vehicle emissions

If your vehicle is battery-powered (electric), use the electricity carbon calculator below.
If it is non-electric (so powered by fossil fuels -burned in a internal combustion engine-), you'll need to calculate it here below.
If you are using biofuels in your internal combustion engine, no emissions will result, and you can dismiss this section.
Many public transport vehicles (like buses, airplanes, ...) however use fossil fuels and if you make use of these, you will need to calculate them. That said, the emissions of these vehicles are shared over all passengers using them.

km X l/km X 2,3 kg of CO2/liter of gasoline = kg of CO2 / passengers = kg of CO2 per passenger

km X l/km X 2,6 kg of CO2/liter of diesel = kg of CO2 / passengers = kg of CO2 per passenger

km X l/km X 0,6 kg of CO2/liter of CNG = kg of CO2 / passengers = kg of CO2 per passenger

km X l/km X 2,6 kg of CO2/liter of kerosene = kg of CO2 / passengers = kg of CO2 per passenger

km X l/km X 2,5 kg of CO2/liter of Jet-A fuel = kg of CO2 / passengers = kg of CO2 per passenger

km X l/km X 2,2 kg of CO2/liter of Avgas = kg of CO2 / passengers = kg of CO2 per passenger

Notes

You'll need to look up the average consumption of your vehicle (liter per km) yourself.
For the fuel used by your vehicle: you'll also need to look this up yourself. Do note though that most passenger airplanes use kerosene. Jet-A (jet fuel) and Avgas (aviation gas) are used mainly by small aircraft.
Most large commercial aircraft use about 2000-3000 liter per hour and have a speed of 850 km/h, meaning consumption is about 2,35 to 3,5 liter of kerosine per km (if only 1 passenger is transported).
Most buses use about 26 liter of diesel fuel per 100 km and have about 50 passenger seats.
Most container ships (Panamax class, slow steaming -35 km/h-) consume about 80 tons of number 5 or number 6 fuel oil per day. 80 tons of fuel oil per day equals to 89840 liter/day or hence 3743 liter/hour. At a speed of 35 km/h, this makes for a fuel consumpton of 106 liter/km. These vessels are able to transport up to 53 500 tons of material (divided over 300 to 5000 20 foot containers a.k.a. TEU's).

Electricity carbon calculator

kWh X 0,004 kg CO2/kWh of hydroelectric power = kg of CO2

kWh X 0,006 kg CO2/kWh of nuclear power = kg of CO2

kWh X 0,02 kg CO2/kWh of wind power = kg of CO2

kWh X 0,10 kg CO2/kWh of PV solar panel power = kg of CO2

kWh X 0,6 kg CO2/kWh of natural gas = kg of CO2

kWh X 1 kg CO2/kWh of coal = kg of CO2

Animal-derived food carbon calculator

For the production of several types of food (derived from animals), methane (and thus carbon) has also been emitted by the animals via their digestion process. Given that plants don't have a digestive tract, there are no emissions for plant-based foods. Since the emissions are relatively large, we need to incorporate these as well. We should note that depending on the fodder used, the first or the latter value will need to be used. Also note that only 3 types of animals (and 2 animal products) are presented here. To calculate other animals/animal products (not listed here), use either one of these 3 types (choose the one that is closest to it).

kg of beef (cattle) X 14 to 32 kg CO2 = kg to kg of CO2

kg of porc meat X 3,9 to 10 kg CO2 = kg to kg of CO2

kg of chicken meat X 3,7 to 6,9 kg CO2 = kg to kg of CO2

kg of milk (from cattle) X 0,8 to 1,3 kg CO2 = kg to kg of CO2

kg of eggs (from chickens) X 3,9 to 4,9 kg CO2= kg to kg of CO2

Notes

Figures were obtained from "Livestock Farming Systems and their Environmental Impacts" (Quest magazine, september 2009)

Carbon offset price

The price per kg of CO2 you'll end up paying will depend on the CER (certified emission reductions) supplier you select. The price of these is country-specific, and can range from 5€ to 30€ per ton of CO2.
So, an average price you'll pay is 17€ (or hence 0,017 €/kg of CO2).
Multiply this amount by the CO2 values you've found for your home and transport and you'll have the average total cost for your offsetting.
